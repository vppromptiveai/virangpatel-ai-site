Architecture

How I design telemetry & analytics systems

My default approach is a Fabric-first, lakehouse-backed architecture with ADX/KQL engines and PBIP semantic models. The goal: governed, reusable, and cost-aware platforms.

Patterns I reuse across programs
  • Ingestion (Bronze): Fabric and/or ADF pipelines with schema contracts, retry logic, and freshness checks.
  • Transformation (Silver): KQL + SQL endpoints to standardize entities (User, Device, Incident, Risk Event, Policy, Cluster, SKU).
  • Modeling (Gold): star schemas, Direct Lake-ready tables, materialized views, and calculation groups for PBIP.
  • Visualization: modular PBIP navigation, shared themes, consistent slicers, and RLS/OLS aligned to org structure.
What leaders get from this approach
  • Faster decisions via consolidated, exec-ready dashboards instead of scattered spreadsheets and ad-hoc reports.
  • Lower ADX compute and better performance through pre-aggregations, query optimization, and offloading analytics to Fabric Lakehouse.
  • A repeatable pattern that new regions, orgs, or programs can adopt without reinventing the stack.
